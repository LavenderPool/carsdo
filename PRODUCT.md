# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Primary users are Russian-speaking car shoppers and auto enthusiasts researching new vehicles, trims, prices, photos, crash tests, and test drives before making a shortlist or visiting a dealer. They often arrive from search on a specific model or topic, need to compare quickly, and expect clear, trustworthy information without hype or friction.

Secondary users are internal editors and operators who maintain the catalog, media, and SEO content through the admin area. Their workflow matters, but the default design context for this project is the public CarsDo experience.

## Product Purpose
CarsDo helps people discover and understand new cars in Russia through a content-rich catalog that combines official specifications, pricing context, reviews, crash tests, photos, and model updates in one place. Success means becoming a reliable destination users return to for automotive research and media discovery, not just a one-off SEO landing page.

## Brand Personality
Editorial, trustworthy, informed.

CarsDo should feel like a confident automotive reference: clear-headed, current, and useful. The tone should favor signal over noise, helping users feel oriented and informed while preserving a sense of enthusiasm for new models and industry updates.

## Anti-references
- Clickbait automotive content farms that trade trust for aggressive headlines, cluttered pages, and shallow summaries.
- Generic SEO catalog pages that feel mass-produced, with weak hierarchy, interchangeable layouts, and no editorial point of view.
- Flashy portal-style automotive UIs that lean on loud gradients, decorative chrome, and visual noise instead of credibility and clarity.

## Design Principles
- Lead with editorial trust. Every page should feel researched, current, and dependable before it feels promotional or dramatic.
- Make discovery effortless. Users should be able to scan model information, media, and supporting context quickly, especially when arriving from search on a single car or topic.
- Balance utility with enthusiasm. The product should support serious research while still feeling connected to the excitement of new cars and industry coverage.
- Let content carry authority. Specs, prices, crash tests, reviews, and photos should do the persuasive work; the interface should organize and elevate them, not compete with them.
- Improve the public surface first. The public CarsDo experience is the default brand context; internal admin workflows can use more task-oriented product patterns when handled separately.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA across the public experience. Prioritize strong text contrast, visible keyboard focus states, readable type for dense Russian-language content, and reduced-motion support for non-essential animation. Important information such as prices, safety ratings, and navigation should never depend on color alone, and dense catalog pages should remain understandable on smaller screens and for users with low vision.
